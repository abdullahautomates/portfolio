01 Overview

The problem with
manual sales

FASSET's inbound leads were falling through the cracks. Researching prospects, writing personalised outreach, assembling pitch decks, chasing signatures — every stage demanded hours of human attention per lead. The team was reactive, inconsistent, and perpetually behind.

The goal was to compress the entire top-of-funnel into minutes without sacrificing the personalised experience that differentiates a real pitch from a generic blast. Every touchpoint needed to feel crafted — even when nothing human created it.

What was built is a fully autonomous acquisition pipeline, with a custom CRM tracking every lead in real time through each stage. The system handles outreach, pitch creation, call scheduling, outcome analysis, and post-call follow-through — adapting its behaviour based on what the AI determines happened on the call.

02 The Story

Before, during,
and after

Before
A sales team losing hours before the first email was sent

Every inbound lead started the same way: someone on the team would open LinkedIn, find the company, try to piece together who this person actually was — their background, their business, what they might care about. Then they'd write an email. Then they'd build a pitch deck, pulling slides, adjusting figures, rewriting copy to feel relevant.

On a good day, a lead got a response within a few hours. On a busy day — multiple leads coming in, calls to run, deals to close — a new prospect might wait until the following morning. By then, the conversation had already gone cold. They'd moved on, or a competitor had already reached them.

The team wasn't slow. The process was. Every lead required the same sequence of manual steps, and there was no way to run those steps faster without hiring more people to do them.

Six hours from form submission to first contact — and that was on a good day. The pipeline wasn't leaking leads. It was haemorrhaging them.
The shift
Speed isn't the goal. Removing the delay entirely is.

The first instinct is to hire faster researchers, better writers, more salespeople. But that scales linearly with cost — and it still doesn't solve the fundamental problem, which is that humans take time to do things that don't require human judgement.

Researching a company online, compiling what matters, writing an email that sounds personal, generating a deck with the right framing — none of these steps actually require a person. They require information, pattern recognition, and good writing. All of which a well-designed system handles faster and more consistently than any team member having their fourth coffee of the day.

The human value in FASSET's sales process was in the conversation — the call, the relationship, the negotiation. Everything before that call was logistics. The decision was to automate the logistics completely and let the team do only what required them.

Sequencing the emails took more deliberation than the automation itself. Sending the welcome, pitch deck, and NDA CTA within minutes of each other felt like spam and overwhelmed leads. Spacing them hours apart broke the momentum entirely. After testing and discussion, 15-minute intervals between each touchpoint became the standard — enough breathing room to feel considered, short enough to keep the lead warm.

The insight wasn't "use AI to help with sales." It was "identify exactly which parts of sales don't require a human, and remove the human from those parts entirely."
After
A lead fills the form. Ten seconds later, the pipeline is already running.

Now, the moment a prospect submits the form, the system is already working. Their LinkedIn is being scraped, their company's news is being pulled, their social activity is being read. By the time a human on the team knows the lead exists, a personalised first email has already landed in the prospect's inbox.

By the time the prospect reads that email, a custom pitch deck built around their specific business has been generated and is waiting. A booking link is attached. The CRM already has their full record. The team's only job is to show up to the call prepared — because everything else has already been handled.

After the call, the system takes over again: analyses the transcript, determines the outcome, sends the NDA or the follow-through email, and keeps chasing until there's a resolution. The team closes deals. The system handles everything around the deals.

One edge case required a separate solution: clients who prefer to sign physically. A second option in the NDA email gives them a link to upload a scanned copy instead. An OCR layer then extracts the document and verifies it's actually signed — because early on, leads were occasionally sending back the original unsigned file, which would have silently passed through without the check.

The pipeline tripled in capacity without adding a single person. The team didn't get faster — they got to stop doing the things that were slowing them down.
03 The System

Every stage, automated

Eight distinct nodes. Two conditional branches. One continuous loop — running without human intervention unless an outcome demands it.

Step 02
Deep research — built around the person, not just the company
Before a single word of outreach is written, the system pulls and synthesises: LinkedIn profile and recent activity, company news and funding history, personal social media posts and interests, public interviews and speaking engagements, recent press coverage, and professional history. Every piece of this feeds directly into the email copy and pitch deck — so the outreach feels like it came from someone who spent an afternoon preparing, not a tool that spent ten seconds.

05 Custom CRM

Built to track
every move

The automation is only half the system. Without visibility there's no accountability — so a purpose-built CRM was designed and deployed alongside the funnel, tightly integrated with every automated stage.

Every lead enters the CRM the moment the form fires. Every pipeline action — email sent, deck generated, call booked, NDA dispatched, follow-up triggered — writes back to the lead record automatically. Manual overrides by team members persist without disrupting automation state. Nothing gets lost between the two.

The result is a single source of truth with multiple views, role-based access control, a live activity feed, task assignments, and completion gates — all in one place.

OVERVIEW
Pipeline dashboard. Top-level stats — total leads, call rate, active NDAs, deal velocity, and conversion at each stage — updated live as the automation runs. Visible at a glance from any access level.
ACCESS
Role-based access levels. Admins have full control — reassign, delete, configure. Sales reps see only their leads. Viewers get read-only access to reports and pipeline status. Each role sees a tailored interface.
LIVE
Real-time updates feed. A live activity stream surfaces every automation action and manual edit — with timestamps, actor attribution, and the exact trigger. Nothing happens silently.
ASSIGN
Lead assignment and load balancing. Leads are distributed automatically by round-robin or manually reassigned by admins. Each rep's active pipeline load is visible from the overview, preventing bottlenecks.
TASKS
Per-lead task completions. Checklist-based task tracking inside each lead record. Human-in-the-loop actions — reviewing a transcript, approving an NDA draft — are gated until marked complete, blocking automation from advancing prematurely.
SORT
Multi-field filtering and sorting. Filter by stage, rep, outcome, date range, deal size, or NDA status. Sort any column ascending or descending. Switch between Kanban board and sortable lead list view in one click.
